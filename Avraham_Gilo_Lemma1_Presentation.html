The Paper

Avraham & Gilo (2025)

Insurance Collusion and Imperfect Competition when Insurers Increase Risk

📋 Model Setup

  • Wealth W, Harm H, probability P
  • Loading factor q ≥ 1 (supra-competitive)
  • Risk-averse consumers: U''(A) < 0
  • Arrow-Pratt: Ra(A) = −U''(A)/U'(A)
  • Coverage α* ∈ [0,1] maximizes EU

📐 Lemma 1

  • (i) Single-peaked: If Ra decreases fast enough, ∃ P̄ maximizing demand
  • (ii) Monotone: If Ra is not too decreasing, demand falls with P
  • Proof: Implicit differentiation → Eq.(17)
  • Limit arguments at P→0 and P→1
Mathematics

Key Equations Verified

FOC (Eq. 1)

−(1−P)·q·U'(W₁) + (1−qP)·U'(W₂) = 0

Derivative (Eq. 17)

dα*/dP = [(1−P)αqH(Ra₁−Ra₂) + (q−1)/(1−qP)] / [−(1−P)H(qP·Ra₁ + (1−qP)·Ra₂)]

CRRA Specialization

U(A) = A^(1−γ) / (1−γ) Ra(A) = γ / A (DARA)

Critical γ Threshold

γ_crit = (q−1)·W·(W−H(1−α)) / (α·q·H²·(1−α))

All 6 equations (14–19) from the Appendix verified computationally in the notebook.
